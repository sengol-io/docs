Sengol's team features let you bring your entire AI governance function into a single shared workspace. With a Team or Enterprise plan, security leads, compliance officers, legal teams, and AI engineers can collaborate on assessments, share risk findings, track remediation progress together, and present unified reporting to stakeholders — without duplicating work or losing context.

## Plan requirements

<CardGroup cols={2}>

</Note>

## Inviting team members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your team settings">
    From your dashboard, navigate to **Settings → Team Members**. You'll see a list of current members and any pending invitations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send an invitation">
    Click **Invite Member** and enter the email address of the person you want to add. You can invite multiple people at once by entering additional addresses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The invitee accepts and joins">
    The invited user receives an email with a link to accept the invitation. If they already have a Sengol account, they'll be added to your team immediately after accepting. If not, they'll be prompted to create an account — the process takes under a minute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access to shared projects is granted automatically">
    Once a user joins your team, they can immediately view and contribute to all shared projects in your workspace. There's no additional step required to grant project access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Shared projects

On Team and Enterprise plans, projects are shared across all team members. This means your whole team is working from a single, consistent record of your AI systems' risk history. Within a shared project, any team member can:

- **View all assessments** — including full results, scores, category breakdowns, and evidence insights from any assessment run in the project
- **Run new assessments** — any team member can start an assessment and save it to the shared project, contributing to the project's history
- **Track compliance status and risk score history** — the project dashboard shows score trends over time, making it easy to demonstrate progress to leadership
- **Export reports and download evidence** — team members on Pro ($49/mo) or above can generate PDF reports and Excel exports from any assessment in the shared project

<Tip>
  Assign different team members to own specific risk categories — for example, your security lead owns Security Risk, your compliance officer owns Compliance Risk, and your engineering lead owns Performance and Operational Risk. This makes remediation accountable and traceable.
</Tip>

## AI Review Board (Team plan and above)

The **AI Review Board** is a Team plan exclusive feature that adds a second layer of analysis to your assessments. Instead of relying on a single model evaluation, the AI Review Board runs your assessment through **multiple AI models independently**, each evaluating your risk profile from a different analytical perspective.

Sengol then synthesizes the results into a unified finding, surfacing areas of agreement — where all models flag the same risk — as well as areas of divergence, where different models reach different conclusions. This broader analytical view helps you:

- Identify risks that a single-model evaluation might underweight
- Increase confidence in Critical and High severity findings before escalating to leadership
- Surface edge cases specific to your industry or use case that benefit from multi-model scrutiny

The AI Review Board results appear alongside your standard assessment results. No additional setup is required — it runs automatically on every assessment for Team and Enterprise plan workspaces.
