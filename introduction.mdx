Sengol is a pre-deployment AI risk assessment and compliance platform designed for the professionals accountable for AI governance: CISOs, risk officers, legal counsel, and C-suite executives. Instead of discovering risk after a system is live — when remediation is expensive and regulatory exposure is real — Sengol lets you quantify, prioritize, and document risk before you invest. Every score is grounded in evidence drawn from more than 100,000 real-world security incidents, sourced from 200+ authoritative references across 40+ jurisdictions.

## What Sengol does

Sengol translates the complexity of AI risk into structured, actionable outputs that your team can act on and your auditors can trust:

- **Pre-deployment risk scoring** — Assess any AI system before it goes live using a structured questionnaire that covers your model type, data flows, deployment environment, and organizational controls.
- **Compliance validation** — Map your AI system's risk profile against nine major regulatory frameworks simultaneously, identifying gaps before they become findings.
- **Evidence-based insights** — Every risk flag is backed by real incident data, not generic checklists. Scores reflect what actually goes wrong in systems similar to yours.
- **Team collaboration** — Invite colleagues, share assessments across your organization, and maintain a living record of AI risk decisions for governance and audit purposes.
- **Exportable audit-ready reports** — Generate PDF and Excel reports that capture your risk scores, compliance posture, and mitigation roadmap — formatted for board presentations, audits, and due diligence.

## Supported compliance frameworks

Sengol maps your risk profile against the frameworks your legal, compliance, and audit teams already work with. Supported frameworks include:

- **EU AI Act** — Obligations under the EU's risk-based AI regulation, including prohibited practices and high-risk system requirements
- **GDPR** — Data protection and privacy requirements for AI systems processing personal data of EU residents
- **CCPA** — California Consumer Privacy Act obligations for AI systems handling California residents' data
- **HIPAA** — Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act requirements for AI systems in healthcare settings
- **SOC 2** — Trust Services Criteria relevant to AI systems — availability, confidentiality, security, and processing integrity
- **ISO 27001** — Information security management controls applicable to AI system development and operations
- **ISO 42001** — The international standard specifically for AI management systems
- **PCI-DSS** — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard requirements for AI systems in financial environments
- **NIST AI RMF** — National Institute of Standards and Technology AI Risk Management Framework govern, map, measure, and manage functions
