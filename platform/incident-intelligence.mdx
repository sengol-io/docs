Sengol's Incident Intelligence feed keeps you informed as the AI threat landscape evolves. Rather than discovering that a vulnerability class or attack pattern has emerged only after it affects you, the feed surfaces new incidents, exploits, breaches, and regulatory violations in real time — matched to your industry, tech stack, and deployment profile so the most relevant signals reach you first.

## What you can do with Incident Intelligence

<Accordion title="Monitor emerging AI threats relevant to your industry">
  Filter the incident feed by sector, model type, or deployment environment to surface the threats most likely to affect systems like yours. New incidents are added continuously, so your threat picture stays current without manual research.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Understand how similar systems have failed">

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Feed current threat data into new risk assessments">

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Export incident data for internal reporting">

</Accordion>

## Accessing the feed

Navigate to **Sengol Realtime Alerts** at [https://sengol.ai/news](https://sengol.ai/news) to open the full incident feed. You can also reach the feed from the **dashboard sidebar** under the Realtime Alerts section.

Use the filters at the top of the feed to narrow incidents by:
- **Industry** — e.g., healthcare, financial services, public sector
- **Incident type** — e.g., data breach, adversarial attack, regulatory violation, model failure
- **Framework** — e.g., EU AI Act, GDPR, HIPAA
- **Date range** — view recent incidents or search historical records
- **Jurisdiction** — filter by country or regulatory region

## How incident data improves your assessments

Sengol's risk scoring is not based on static rule sets. Every question in the Risk Assessment questionnaire is **weighted by evidence frequency** from the incident database — meaning questions covering failure modes that appear more often in documented incidents carry greater influence on your score.

As new incidents are recorded, these weights update in real time. An emerging attack pattern that suddenly appears across multiple industries will increase the weight of related questions and may shift risk scores across existing assessments, prompting you to reassess where new evidence warrants it.

This evidence loop means your risk scores reflect the actual threat environment your AI system operates in — not a fixed checklist frozen at a point in time.
