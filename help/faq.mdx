This page answers the most common questions about Sengol — from running your first assessment and understanding your risk scores, to navigating compliance frameworks and choosing the right plan for your team.

## Getting Started

<Accordion title="How long does a risk assessment take?">
  A quick assessment typically takes **5–10 minutes**, while a comprehensive assessment runs **15–20 minutes**. Sengol's questionnaire adapts dynamically to your answers, so the time you spend reflects the complexity and scope of your AI system. The more detail you provide, the more precise your results will be.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need a credit card to sign up?">
  No. The Free plan requires no credit card — simply [create an account](https://sengol.ai/auth/register) and start assessing right away. Paid plans (Pro, Team, and Enterprise) also come with a **14-day free trial** that requires no credit card, so you can explore premium features without any commitment.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What information do I need before running an assessment?">
  To get the most accurate results, have the following details on hand before you begin:

  - **Industry** — the sector your AI system operates in (e.g., healthcare, finance, retail)
  - **Use case** — what the AI system does and how it's used
  - **Tech stack** — the models, frameworks, and infrastructure involved
  - **Data sensitivity** — the types of personal or regulated data your system processes
  - **Team size** — the number of people involved in building or operating the system
  - **Existing controls** — any security, compliance, or governance measures already in place

  You don't need to have all answers ready — Sengol's questionnaire guides you step by step.
</Accordion>

## Risk Scores

<Accordion title="What do the risk scores mean?">
  Every Sengol assessment produces a **risk score from 0 to 100**, where a higher score indicates higher risk. Scores are calculated across four categories:

  - **Performance** — reliability, accuracy, and failure modes
  - **Security** — vulnerability exposure and attack surfaces
  - **Compliance** — alignment with applicable regulations and standards
  - **Operational** — deployment readiness and monitoring maturity

  The scoring methodology is grounded in **NIST AI RMF**, **ISO 42001**, and the **EU AI Act**, and each score comes with prioritised, actionable insights so you know exactly what to address first.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How accurate are the risk assessments?">
  Sengol's assessments achieve an **89.3% prediction accuracy**, validated against evidence drawn from **78,767+ real-world AI incidents** across more than 200 sources. This empirical foundation means your scores reflect patterns seen in production systems, not just theoretical checklists.

  That said, no automated tool replaces expert judgement. Sengol's assessments are designed to **complement professional legal, security, and compliance analysis** — not substitute for it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How often should I run assessments?">
  We recommend treating assessments as a living practice rather than a one-time event. A good baseline is:

  - **Quarterly** — as a regular governance checkpoint
  - **After architecture changes** — if you retrain, fine-tune, or significantly modify your AI system
  - **When entering new jurisdictions** — different regions bring different regulatory obligations
  - **When new regulations come into force** — your compliance posture may shift even without product changes
  - **After an incident or near-miss** — to understand how your risk profile has changed

  You can save all assessments to projects to track your risk posture over time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can my score change without me changing anything?">
  Yes. Sengol continuously incorporates new real-world incidents into its evidence database and monitors regulatory updates across 40+ jurisdictions. As the threat and compliance landscape evolves, your risk profile may shift — even if your system hasn't changed. This is intentional: it ensures your scores always reflect the **current state of the world**, not just the state of your system at the time you last answered the questionnaire.

  <Tip>
    Re-running a quarterly assessment is the best way to stay on top of emerging risks without waiting for something to go wrong.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

## Compliance

<Accordion title="Which compliance frameworks does Sengol cover?">
  Sengol maps your risk assessment results to the following frameworks and regulations:

  - **EU AI Act**
  - **GDPR**
  - **CCPA**
  - **HIPAA**
  - **SOC 2**
  - **ISO 27001**
  - **ISO 42001**
  - **PCI-DSS**
  - **NIST AI RMF**

  Coverage spans **40+ jurisdictions** including the EU, US, and APAC regions, so whether you're preparing for a European regulatory audit or a US enterprise security review, Sengol surfaces the specific controls and gaps that matter.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does Sengol provide legal advice?">
  No. Sengol provides **risk assessment guidance** based on its evidence database and compliance mappings, but this does not constitute legal advice. You should always engage qualified legal counsel when making compliance decisions, especially in regulated industries or high-risk AI deployments.

  <Note>
    Sengol is designed to make conversations with your legal and compliance teams faster and more informed — not to replace them.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What jurisdictions are covered?">
  Sengol covers **40+ jurisdictions**, with deep coverage across:

  - **European Union** — including EU AI Act, GDPR, and national implementing legislation
  - **United States** — including CCPA, HIPAA, NIST frameworks, and sector-specific rules
  - **Asia-Pacific** — including Singapore, Australia, Japan, and other key markets

  Coverage is continuously expanded as new AI-specific regulations come into force globally.
</Accordion>

## Plans & Features

<Accordion title="Can I upgrade or downgrade my plan?">
  Yes — you can change your plan at any time from your [dashboard](https://sengol.ai/dashboard).

  - **Upgrades** take effect immediately, giving you instant access to additional assessments, projects, and features.
  - **Downgrades** take effect at the end of your current billing period, so you retain access to your current plan's features until the cycle ends.

  View all plan options on the [pricing page](https://sengol.ai/pricing).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I save assessments to projects?">
  Yes. All paid plans (Pro, Team, and Enterprise) allow you to create projects and save your full assessment history within them. Projects let you organise assessments by product, team, or regulatory scope and track how your risk posture changes over time.

  - **Free** — up to 2 projects (history not saved)
  - **Pro** — up to 20 projects with full assessment history
  - **Team / Enterprise** — unlimited projects with full history and multi-user access
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is my assessment data private?">
  Yes. All assessment data is **encrypted at rest and in transit**, private to your account (or your team, on Team and Enterprise plans), and is **never shared with third parties**. Sengol does not use your assessment data to train models or benchmark against other organisations without your explicit consent.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does the API work?">
  The **REST API** is included on the **Enterprise plan** and allows you to integrate Sengol risk assessments programmatically into your CI/CD pipelines, internal tooling, or governance workflows. An API add-on is also available for Growth and Enterprise API tiers.

  To get started with the API, [contact the Sengol team](mailto:support@sengol.ai) or refer to the API documentation once you have an Enterprise account.
</Accordion>
